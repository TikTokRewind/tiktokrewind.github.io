# TikTokRewind — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-05-16
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. Summary

TikTokRewind is a content channel, not a service. It does not collect personal data from viewers. It does not have user accounts. It does not place cookies, run analytics scripts, or operate any tracker infrastructure. This policy explains what little data the operator does handle and the legal basis for it.

## 2. Data the operator collects

The only personal data the operator processes is:

1. **Content sent to the contact email (`tiktokrewind@proton.me`):** when you email the operator (e.g., to request an opt-out under the Terms of Service), the operator sees your email address and message content. This data is retained only as long as needed to respond and act on your request.
2. **Publicly visible creator information used in editorial updates:** for each post, the operator researches the original creator's public profile and current activity. This information is drawn from public sources (news coverage, the creator's own current public profiles, Wikipedia, etc.) and is reproduced in editorial form in the "where are they now" update. The operator does not access private accounts, deleted profiles, or non-public records.
3. **An internal denylist of opted-out parties** (Terms of Service §4): if you opt out, the operator records your identifying details (creator handle, contact email) so that your content is not re-posted in the future. This denylist is retained indefinitely because opt-out is permanent.

## 3. Data the operator does not collect

- Viewer identities, IP addresses, browser fingerprints, or device data
- Analytics on individual viewers
- Engagement metadata beyond the aggregate counts provided by the destination platforms
- Any data from any account other than the operator's own brand accounts

## 4. Third-party platforms and APIs

The Channel publishes through and runs on third-party platforms and APIs:

- **TikTok** (post publishing, basic analytics) — see TikTok's privacy policy: https://www.tiktok.com/legal/privacy-policy
- **YouTube / Google** (Shorts publishing, basic analytics) — see Google's privacy policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
- **Meta / Instagram** (Reels publishing, basic analytics) — see Meta's privacy policy: https://www.facebook.com/privacy/policy/
- **Anthropic** (editorial-draft generation only — the operator queries the API for "where are they now" research drafts; no viewer data is sent to Anthropic) — see Anthropic's privacy policy: https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy

When you interact with the Channel's posts on any of those platforms, those platforms' privacy policies govern that interaction. The operator receives only the engagement aggregates those platforms expose to publishers.

## 5. Cookies and trackers

The Channel does not operate a website. It does not place cookies. It does not run analytics scripts. The third-party platforms where the Channel publishes have their own cookie and tracker behavior governed by their policies.

## 6. Children's privacy

The Channel is published on platforms with their own minimum-age requirements (typically 13+). The Channel does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe content depicting a minor warrants removal under the Terms of Service §4 opt-out flow, please contact the operator.

## 7. Your rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data the operator holds about you. To exercise these rights (or to request your data be removed from the denylist, which itself contains personal data), contact the operator at **tiktokrewind@proton.me**. The operator will respond in good faith within a reasonable timeframe.

For creator-content opt-out, see Terms of Service §4 — the procedure is the same.

## 8. Data security

Operator-side data (the contact email inbox and the denylist) is stored on Proton's encrypted infrastructure and an encrypted local database respectively. Access is limited to the operator.

## 9. Changes

This policy may be updated. The "Effective date" at the top reflects the current version. Material changes will be reflected in the Channel's bio on its platform accounts.
